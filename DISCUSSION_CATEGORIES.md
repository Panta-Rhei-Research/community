# Discussion Categories

Use this category model for GitHub Organization Discussions:

1. **Announcements**
   - Format: Announcement
   - Purpose: release updates, Research Notes, publication releases, major site changes.

2. **Start Here / Orientation**
   - Format: Q&A
   - Purpose: first questions, where to read, where to post, how to navigate.

3. **Ask About the Framework**
   - Format: Q&A
   - Purpose: conceptual questions about Category tau and the overall program.

4. **Research Agenda**
   - Format: Q&A or open-ended
   - Purpose: Problem Ledger, Recovery Requirements, Kernel/Model/Reality, Construction Roadmap.

5. **Corpus & Construction Spine**
   - Format: Q&A
   - Purpose: Registry, Construction Spine, dependency paths, Corpus navigation.

6. **TauLib / Formalization**
   - Format: Q&A
   - Purpose: Lean, formal statements, modules, build issues, formalization questions.

7. **Results & World Readouts**
   - Format: Q&A
   - Purpose: result status, Landmark Results, World Readouts, Problem Ledger Answers.

8. **Predictions & Falsification**
   - Format: Q&A
   - Purpose: numerical predictions, falsification paths, challenge threads.

9. **Research Notes**
   - Format: open-ended
   - Purpose: discussion of published Research Notes.

10. **Impact & Public-Good Briefings**
    - Format: open-ended
    - Purpose: conditional impact, Research Briefings, Public-Good Briefings.

11. **Corrections / Errata Candidates**
    - Format: Q&A or open-ended
    - Purpose: possible errors before they become formal Issues.

12. **Review Offers / Domain Expertise**
    - Format: open-ended
    - Purpose: offers to inspect a bounded area of the program.

13. **Meta / Infrastructure**
    - Format: open-ended
    - Purpose: GitHub org, website structure, taxonomy, search, tooling, workflows.

De-emphasize generic defaults such as General, Polls, and Show and tell where possible.
