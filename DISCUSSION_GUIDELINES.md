# Discussion Guidelines

Panta Rhei Discussions are for structured engagement with an open research program.

## Core principle

Participation does not imply endorsement.

You may question, challenge, inspect, correct, or contribute without accepting the framework.

## What we value

- specificity;
- traceability;
- correction;
- clear distinctions between internal claims and external acceptance;
- domain expertise;
- honest uncertainty;
- reproducible objections;
- patient questions;
- careful reading.

## How to ask a useful question

Include:

- the page or artifact you are reading;
- the concept or claim you are asking about;
- what you already checked;
- what would help you proceed.

## How to challenge a claim

A useful challenge names:

- the exact claim;
- the supporting page or artifact;
- the suspected failure point;
- whether the issue is formal, empirical, conceptual, source-related, or rhetorical;
- what evidence would resolve the issue.

## What to avoid

- personal attacks;
- drive-by dismissals;
- culture-war framing;
- vague accusations;
- claims without page/artifact references;
- treating internal program status as external acceptance;
- asking others to defend claims not made by the program.

## Moderation

Maintainers may move, relabel, close, or lock discussions when they are off-topic, duplicate, unconstructive, or violate the code of conduct.

Strong criticism is welcome. Non-specific hostility is not.
